# Fluxora Business Context

**Status:** Demo and pilot grounding file  
**Last reviewed:** 2026-07-16

## Company

Fluxora is a fictional data and AI infrastructure platform company. Its portfolio covers lakehouse storage and query workloads, data-pipeline operations, ML model serving, data governance, and a StreamSync product or capability whose final taxonomy must be confirmed from approved product sources.

## Target customers and buyers

Primary customers are mid-market and enterprise organizations modernizing data platforms, migrating from legacy warehouses, or building data and AI pipelines.

Typical buyers and evaluators include:

- Chief Data Officer
- VP of Engineering
- Head of Data Engineering
- Director of Platform Engineering
- Data and platform engineering leaders
- Technical evaluators and senior data engineers

## Product taxonomy

Use these official names and capitalization:

- Lakehouse Core
- PipelineOps
- ModelServe
- Governance Suite
- StreamSync

Do not infer StreamSync's product category, packaging, availability, or capabilities without an approved FAQ, PRD, roadmap, or one-pager.

## Sales stages

Use this stage vocabulary:

1. Discovery
2. Technical Validation
3. POC
4. Business Case
5. Negotiation
6. Closed Won
7. Closed Lost

Do not treat stage progression as proof that technical or commercial risks are resolved.

## Core Agent outcomes

The Agent should reduce repetitive work and surface cross-record context by helping SEs:

- Prepare discovery conversations
- Understand live Opportunity posture
- Review POC success criteria and outcomes
- Draft grounded RFP and security responses
- Retrieve product and competitive guidance
- Identify contradictions, missing evidence, and the next best action

## Evidence policy

- Salesforce is authoritative for live CRM facts.
- Approved Fluxora documents are authoritative for internal product, security, compliance, pricing, roadmap, and competitive claims.
- Current official public sources are authoritative for current external company and competitor facts.
- Inference must be labeled and must not be presented as customer-confirmed evidence.

## Writing defaults

- Lead with the decision-relevant point.
- Be concise, specific, and evidence-based.
- Separate facts, inference, and recommendations when the distinction matters.
- Use cautious language for incomplete evidence.
- Never make an unapproved customer commitment.
